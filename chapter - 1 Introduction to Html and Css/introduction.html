Spaghetti Carbonara

A classic Italian pasta dish with creamy eggs, pancetta, and cheese

Home » Recipes » Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a simple yet indulgent pasta dish that hails from Rome. Made with just a few fresh ingredients — spaghetti, pancetta, eggs, Parmesan cheese, and freshly cracked black pepper — this dish delivers rich flavors and a luscious creamy texture without any cream. It’s perfect for quick dinners or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large free-range eggs
  • 3/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Sea salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Equipment Needed

  • Large pot for boiling pasta
  • Skillet or frying pan
  • Mixing bowl
  • Tongs or pasta fork
  • Grater for cheese
  • Whisk or fork

Cooking Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente according to package instructions (usually about 8-10 minutes).
  2. While pasta cooks, heat the skillet over medium heat. Add diced pancetta and cook until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Add minced garlic for the last 1 minute if using. Remove from heat.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper until well combined.
  4. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
  5. Add the hot pasta immediately to the skillet with pancetta (off the heat to avoid scrambling eggs). Quickly pour in the egg and cheese mixture while tossing the pasta vigorously with tongs to coat each strand.
  6. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time as needed to create a silky smooth sauce. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs gently.
  7. Season with salt and extra pepper if desired. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley and extra Parmesan.

Chef's Tips

  • Use fresh, high-quality eggs and cheese for the best flavor.
  • Do not add cream; the sauce should be creamy from the eggs and cheese only.
  • Be careful when adding the egg mixture to hot pasta — tossing quickly is key to prevent scrambled eggs.
  • Guanciale (cured pork jowl) is traditional, but pancetta or bacon works well.
  • Save some pasta water before draining — it helps loosen the sauce and create a perfect texture.
